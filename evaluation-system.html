Bottom Line Up Front

  • We now have a broader and still manageable candidate universe of 12 communities, which is a practical upper limit for the research and phone-screen phase.
  • The candidate set still splits naturally into full-continuum / stronger aging-in-place communities versus assisted-living-centered / more limited-continuum options.
  • For Parkinson’s, care continuum depth + transfer thresholds + nighttime response + internal transition path matter more than amenities.
  • Our plan remains to use a two-round process: initial phone screening → targeted tours, followed by shorter finalist visits with Mom.
  • Our practical goal is now to narrow the list first to 5–6 tour candidates, then to 2–3 finalists.

3. Instructions for the Facility Interview/Tour/Scoring Phase

Use this system in sequence to ensure the decision is both clinical and personal, not just intuitive:

  1. Review the side-by-side comparison matrix
  2. Review pre-filled facility intelligence notes
  3. Conduct initial phone screens before scheduling tours
  4. Use the evaluation form during interviews and tours to capture answers and ranking scores
  5. Transcribe the scoring values from your interview sheets to the JSON data entry block at the bottom of this document
  6. Run the “Score Senior Care Communities” script to calculate the final scoring values and produce the ranking results
  7. Review the final rankings and then reconcile them against emotional fit, location practicality, and Mom’s preferences before making the final selection

Recommended narrowing workflow:

  • 12 → 5–6 by phone screening
  • 5–6 → 2–3 by solo tours / detailed evaluations
  • 2–3 → final choice after shorter follow-up visits with Mom

5. Critical Filter (Parkinson’s Lens)

5.1. Top Tier (Best Fit for Progression)

  • Three Pillars
  • Shorehaven
  • Saint John’s
  • Newcastle Place
  • Harwood / Lutheran

Why:

  • Strongest overall continuum profiles
  • Lower forced-move risk
  • On-site or linked SNF reduces disruption
  • Better likelihood of handling mobility decline, dysphagia, therapy needs, and nighttime support

5.2. Conditional Tier (Depends on Answers to Your Form)

  • Eastcastle Place
  • Tudor Oaks
  • LindenGrove
  • The Arboretum

Key unknowns to verify:

  • Two-person transfer policy
  • Night staffing
  • Medication timing discipline
  • Exact internal transition path when care increases

5.3. Higher Risk Tier (For This Situation)

  • Forest Ridge
  • Library Square
  • The Courtyard at Sussex

Why:

  • Lower or less certain care ceiling
  • No clearly established on-site SNF path
  • Higher likelihood of required external move later

6. Pre-Filled Facility Intelligence Notes

Use this to walk in informed and ask sharper questions.

6.1. Saint John’s On The Lake (Milwaukee – East Side)

Positioning

  • High-end urban Life Plan / CCRC
  • Life Care structure with assisted living and skilled care neighborhoods

Strengths

  • Full continuum (IL → AL → skilled care / rehab)
  • Strong location and strong structural aging-in-place protection
  • Good therapy access

Risks

  • Cost / entrance-fee structure
  • Less campus feel (vertical living)

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • How do you manage medication timing for Parkinson’s?
  • How often are residents reassessed for care level increases?
  • Can assisted living residents remain within Saint John’s if they later require skilled care under the Life Care arrangement?

6.2. Three Pillars (Dousman)

Positioning

  • One of the strongest full-continuum campuses in Waukesha County

Strengths

  • Excellent aging-in-place capability
  • Strong SNF and rehab reputation
  • Good staffing consistency

Risks

  • Distance from Milwaukee
  • More “traditional” feel

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • How many current residents require 2-person assist?
  • How do you manage nighttime toileting support?
  • At what point do you move someone from AL to skilled nursing?

6.3. Newcastle Place (Mequon)

Positioning

  • Newer, upscale Life Plan / CCRC

Strengths

  • Modern facility
  • Strong IL → AL → SNF pipeline
  • On-site skilled nursing and rehab

Risks

  • May prioritize lifestyle over complex care
  • Cost
  • Need to verify depth of advanced Parkinson’s experience

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • What is your threshold for moving someone out of assisted living?
  • Do you regularly manage advanced Parkinson’s cases?
  • Can your assisted living manage 2-person transfers, or does that trigger movement to another level of care?

6.4. Eastcastle Place (Milwaukee)

Positioning

  • Established urban Life Plan / CCRC

Strengths

  • Full continuum
  • Central location
  • Strong health center signal

Risks

  • Older infrastructure
  • Variable staffing consistency
  • Cost

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • What renovations or updates have been made recently?
  • What is your average caregiver tenure?
  • How many residents transition internally versus leaving the campus when care escalates?

6.5. Shorehaven (Oconomowoc)

Positioning

  • Strong clinical / faith-based continuum campus

Strengths

  • Excellent SNF and rehab depth
  • Strong care continuum
  • Lower forced-move risk

Risks

  • Less modern aesthetics
  • Slightly institutional feel
  • Need to verify assisted living care thresholds in practice

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • What percentage of residents transition internally vs externally?
  • How do you handle frequent falls?
  • What is the assisted-living threshold for 2-person assist and swallowing issues?

6.6. Harwood Place / Lutheran Home (Wauwatosa)

Positioning

  • Assisted living / senior living through Harwood Place linked to Lutheran Home rehab, skilled nursing, and memory care

Strengths

  • Excellent location for family access and major medical systems
  • Strong clinical support via linked campus
  • Practical continuum through connected system

Risks

  • Need to verify exactly how transitions occur between Harwood and Lutheran Home
  • Need to verify whether internal transition priority is guaranteed

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • If a Harwood resident declines, what is the actual process for moving into Lutheran Home services?
  • Is priority access or placement guaranteed in writing?
  • Can Harwood assisted living itself manage progressive Parkinson’s decline before Lutheran Home placement is needed?

6.7. Tudor Oaks (Muskego)

Positioning

  • Faith-based full campus continuum community

Strengths

  • Full continuum available
  • Stable organization
  • 24/7 professional medical staff signal

Risks

  • Institutional feel for some
  • Variable modernization
  • Need to verify exact Medicaid / transition path

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • What is your SNF quality rating?
  • How integrated is assisted living with skilled nursing?
  • Can your assisted living manage 2-person transfers and advanced Parkinson’s-related mobility decline?

6.8. LindenGrove (Waukesha)

Positioning

  • Care-focused assisted living / memory care / rehab support campus

Strengths

  • Known for good hands-on care
  • On-site PT/OT/speech signal
  • Strong nursing-support signal

Risks

  • Less clear continuum than classic CCRC
  • Need to verify Medicaid and long-term transition path

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • At what point must a resident leave?
  • Where do residents typically transition to?
  • What is the long-term path if a resident’s needs progress beyond assisted living / memory care?

6.9. The Arboretum Senior Living (Menomonee Falls)

Positioning

  • Independent living + assisted living + memory care + respite

Strengths

  • Better acuity signal than many smaller ALs
  • Supports medication management and non-ambulatory care
  • Worth screening as a dark-horse contender

Risks

  • No clearly established on-site SNF
  • Need to verify transfer threshold and forced-move risk

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • Can your assisted living manage 2-person transfers and non-ambulatory residents in practice?
  • What happens if a resident needs skilled nursing care?
  • Can a resident remain on your campus as care needs increase, or would that require an outside move?

6.10. Forest Ridge Senior Community (Hales Corners)

Positioning

  • Senior apartment community with assisted living services available

Strengths

  • Comfortable apartment model
  • 24-hour staffing / emergency call system
  • May be emotionally easier transition for some residents

Risks

  • Likely lower care ceiling
  • Appears more lifestyle / support hybrid than full clinical continuum

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • What does “assisted living services available” mean operationally?
  • Can residents stay if they develop frequent falls, swallowing issues, or need 2-person assist?
  • If care increases beyond your model, where do residents usually go?

6.11. Library Square Senior Living (West Allis)

Positioning

  • Independent living + assisted living + respite / companion-living model

Strengths

  • Strong location
  • Potentially lower cost
  • Transfer assistance and non-ambulatory support signal

Risks

  • No clearly established on-site SNF pathway
  • Likely higher external-transfer risk than continuum campuses

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • Your materials mention transfer assistance and non-ambulatory care — what are the actual limits?
  • Can assisted living residents stay if they later require 2-person assist?
  • What happens if a resident needs skilled nursing or runs out of private-pay funds?

6.12. The Courtyard at Sussex

Positioning

  • Smaller assisted living / memory care community

Strengths

  • Intimate environment
  • Lower scale
  • May feel less overwhelming

Risks

  • Lower care ceiling
  • Limited medical support
  • No clear on-site SNF path

Facility-Specific Follow-Up Questions

  • Can you support 2-person transfers?
  • What happens if swallowing issues develop?
  • At what point would a resident need to leave your community for higher care?
