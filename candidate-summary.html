Initial List of Candidate Living/Care Facilities

Bottom Line Up Front

  • We now have a strong and practical candidate universe of 12 communities, which is a reasonable upper limit for initial comparison.
  • The list is organized into ranked tiers, with each community intentionally ordered within its tier based on current expected fit.
  • For Parkinson’s progression, continuum depth, transfer thresholds, overnight support, and ability to stay within the same system matter more than amenities alone.
  • Our plan is to use a staged process: phone screening first, then targeted tours, then short finalist visits.
  • Our immediate goal is to narrow the list to 5–6 communities for tours, then reduce to 2–3 finalists.

4. What We Are Trying to Learn in the Initial Calls

The initial phone screening is meant to answer one main question:

Can this community safely support increasing care needs as Parkinson’s progresses, without creating an early forced move?

Key questions include:

  • Can they manage two-person transfers?
  • How quickly is nighttime assistance typically available?
  • At what point would someone need to leave assisted living?
  • Can she remain within the same campus or system if care needs increase?
  • What happens if private-pay funds run down?
  • Is there a Medicaid skilled nursing pathway, if needed later?

6. Current Planning Assumption

Our current likely decision path is:

  1. Phone-screen all 12 communities
  2. Narrow to 5–6 tour candidates
  3. Do detailed tours of those finalists
  4. Reduce to 2–3 strongest options
  5. Then do shorter follow-up visits with Mom

This allows the first pass to focus on:

  • care depth
  • policy
  • staffing
  • financial continuity

Then the later pass can focus more on:

  • comfort
  • atmosphere
  • room feel
  • personal preference
